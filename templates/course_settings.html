About Worksheet Control:

Many instructors prefer to review student performance after only one attempt at completing a worksheet. For example, the first attempt may help an instructor determine where to target further instruction. Worksheet control gives instructors the option to specify what happens after a student has completed a worksheet.

Option 1 indicates that students are able to immediately see their worksheet score and view which questions were answered correctly and incorrectly. The student then as the option to return to the worksheet to retry specific questions. The student can attempt to answer questions as many times as they wish.

Option 2 indicates that students are prevented from immediately reviewing their worksheet score as well as which questions were answered correctly and incorrectly. In this mode, the student cannot review their worksheet results until the instructor has explicitly granted them permission to do so. Once the restriction has been lifted, students may view their worksheet results and reattempt questions they may have answered incorrectly.
